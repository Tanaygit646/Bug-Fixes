Thavil

Origins and History:

The Thavil is a traditional South Indian percussion instrument, commonly used in temple music and folk performances.

Instrument Features:

It is a barrel-shaped drum with a tough skin membrane, played using both hands and sticks.

Musical Role:

Thavil is a key rhythm instrument in Carnatic music, especially in nadaswaram ensembles.

Conclusion

The Thavil remains an integral part of South Indian classical and folk traditions, adding depth and energy to performances.
