Sitar

Origins and History:

The Sitar is a plucked string instrument that originated in the Indian subcontinent and is widely used in Hindustani classical music.

Instrument Features:

It has a long hollow neck, gourd-shaped body, and multiple sympathetic strings, producing a resonant and melodic sound.

Musical Role:

The Sitar is central to Indian classical performances and has been popularized globally by maestros like Ravi Shankar.

Conclusion

The Sitar remains one of the most iconic instruments in Indian music.
