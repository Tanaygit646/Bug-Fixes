Introduction

Raag Bihag is one of the most fundamental and important ragas in Hindustani classical music. It is associated with the early evening and is known for its soothing and devotional character.

Thaat (Parent Scale)

Raag Bihag belongs to the Bilawal Thaat, characterized by the omit of Ma and Ni swaras.

Time of Performance

Traditionally, Raag Bilawal is performed during the **night (9 PM - Midnight)**.

Aaroh (Ascending) & Avaroh (Descending)

Aaroh: N' S G M P N S'

Avaroh: S' N D P M G R S

Vadi & Samvadi (Important Notes)

Vadi (Most prominent note): Ga

Samvadi (Second most important note): Ni

Pakad (Signature Phrase)

N' S G M P, G M G S

Mood & Emotion

Raag Bihag evokes a mood of **devotion, peace, and serenity**. It is considered a "shuddha" (pure) raag and is often associated with spiritual compositions.

Rules & Characteristics

  • It is a Aurav-Sampurna raga, meaning 5 notes in ascent and all 7 notes are used in descent.
  • It has a majestic, expansive feel, making it a popular choice for both vocal and instrumental performances.
